The most famous paintings of all time

Painting is an ancient medium and even with the introduction of photography, film and digital technology, it still has remained a persistent mode of expression.

Leonardo Da Vinci, Mona Lisa

Leonardo da Vinci's painting "Mona Lisa" is one of the most famous and iconic artworks in the world. Painted between 1503 and 1519, it is believed to depict Lisa Gherardini, a woman from Florence, Italy. The painting is renowned for its captivating and enigmatic portrayal of a woman, with a mysterious smile that has fascinated viewers for centuries. "Mona Lisa" is an oil painting on a poplar wood panel, measuring approximately 77 cm x 53 cm. It is housed in the Louvre Museum in Paris, France, where it has been displayed since 1797.

Johannes Vermeer, Girl with a Pearl Earring

"Girl with a Pearl Earring" is a renowned painting created by the Dutch Golden Age artist Johannes Vermeer. Painted around 1665, the artwork is an exquisite example of Vermeer's mastery of light, composition, and subtle details. The painting depicts a young woman, often referred to as "the girl," with a captivating gaze directed toward the viewer. She wears a turban, and a large pearl earring hangs from her earlobe, which is the focal point of the painting. The girl is portrayed against a dark background, emphasizing her luminous complexion and the brilliance of the pearl.

Vincent van Gogh, The Starry Night

"The Starry Night" is a renowned painting created by the Dutch post-impressionist artist Vincent van Gogh in 1889. It is considered one of his most famous and iconic works of art, celebrated for its vivid colors, swirling brushstrokes, and emotional depth. Van Gogh painted "The Starry Night" during his time at the Saint-Paul-de-Mausole asylum in Saint-Rémy-de-Provence, France, where he sought treatment for his mental health. The painting depicts a nighttime scene with a tumultuous sky filled with swirling stars, a radiant crescent moon, and a small village with cypress trees in the foreground.

Georges Seurat, A Sunday Afternoon on the Island of La Grande Jatte

"A Sunday Afternoon on the Island of La Grande Jatte" is a famous painting created by French post-impressionist artist Georges Seurat between 1884 and 1886. It is considered one of Seurat's most notable and influential works, showcasing his innovative pointillist technique and meticulous attention to detail. The painting depicts a scene at the leisurely park of the Île de la Grande Jatte, located on the River Seine in Paris. Seurat meticulously arranged over 50 figures in the composition, capturing a snapshot of the diverse Parisian society enjoying their Sunday afternoon. Men, women, children, and even animals are portrayed engaged in various activities such as strolling, sitting, boating, and socializing.
